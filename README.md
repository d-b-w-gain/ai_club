# AI Club

A casual club for learning about AI through shared experiments, practical examples, and open discussion.

The club is deliberately broad. We welcome people who are excited, skeptical, experienced, new, practical, creative, technical, or just curious. The point is not to promote one view of AI. The point is to compare experiences, try things together, and talk honestly about what AI is useful for, what it changes, and where it raises concerns.

Each meeting has a simple rhythm, loosely borrowed from a good book club:

1. Share what you noticed

   Something you used, read, watched, heard, built, tried, or worried about since the last meeting. This gives new people a way in and helps quieter voices join the conversation.

2. Discuss the current project

   Look at the challenge chosen last time. Someone may demo what they tried, show a partial result, explain what failed, or ask the group for help.

3. Choose the next project

   Pick one small challenge, question, or experiment for next time. GitHub issues are the backlog of possible projects.

## Current Project Ideas

These are examples from what members are currently working on or wondering about:

- Make a short AI-assisted 3D animation with a retro 1950s French New Wave feel.
- Present an open-source collaborative CAD project and discuss where AI may or may not help.
- Explore whether AI can help with house plans, spatial ideas, or design thinking.
- Share examples of productive AI use in creative, technical, or everyday work.
- Discuss how people can use AI without losing authorship, craft, judgment, or trust.

Project ideas should usually start as GitHub issues. That keeps the backlog easy to browse, discuss, and choose from.

## How This Repo Helps

This repository is the club notebook.

- The README introduces the club.
- Issues are the project backlog: ideas, challenges, questions, and things to try next.
- Meeting notes record what happened and what we want to do next.
- Solutions record what people made, tried, learned, or concluded after a meeting challenge.
